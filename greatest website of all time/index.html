What is time travelling?

According to the theory of relativity, “time travel” is the phenomenon where an object experiences a different passage of time than a stationary observer, allowing for travel to the future by experiencing time at a slower rate through high velocity or intense gravity. Essentially, just moving fast can dilate time which can make speeds slower of the stationary, and essentially travel through time. Or going to Jupiter to get stronger gravity to time travel.

This is only for travelling into the future, and the explanation for time travelling into the past is later in the document.

How to time travel into the future?

Easy example:- Elon twins, he sends one to space and one is staying with him. The one who goes to space is going for ten years INSIDE THE SPACE SHIP. Basically, the space ship will experience 10 years in space. The ship accelerates for 5 years and then comes back for the next 5 to Earth. The kid in the ship ages 10 years perfectly, but when he reaches Earth, he will see that his twin is 22 years old, which sounds insane but in theory, this is almost exactly what will happen. This phenomenon is called time dilation, where the intense gravity, or sometimes even speed, the more time goes slow for you, but time for everything else just goes really fast. So when the space twin comes in his ‘10’ years, the earth ages about 22, as time is slower for the ship. The same would happen if you get inside a black hole.

How to time travel into the past?

This one is a bit… difficult to process. According to the theory of relativity, and with some crazy hypothesis, in order to go back in time, you would have to travel faster than light(FTL). Essentially, to be FTL, you would need zero mass and nothing stopping you to accelerate. The speed of light is 299,792,458 m/s or represented by c. Let me explain in better words. The faster you go, the slower the stationary things feel for you. So if we travel at c, they will look stationary, then if we go faster, they would look like they are doing the moves they did before, essentially traveling back in time. This is a hypothesis and there is no evidence or even a way to prove this is physically possible. So if you want to travel back in time, go to space for 50 years, then come back to earth, and see if some smart guy has found a way to travel faster than light, and then travel back in time. The flaws of going back in time? Consequences and paradoxes(not discussed in this document).

Conclusion

Time travelling is a phenomenon where an object experiences a different passage of time, possible by intense gravity or intense speeds. There are two ways to time travel:- Go fast, and go FTL. One is possible with a lot of money, and one is not physically possible. The faster you move, the slower the other stationary objects feel.



Measuring something? Not so fast

Measurement can get real hard. How? You only have to run 3.1 miles to go 5 kms, easy right? Not in a million years. Measurement is way harder. Try measuring a photon using double slits. You’ll give up quite easily, and I just know it.

Schrodinger’s Cat:-

Who is Schrodinger, and who is his cat? Schrodinger was an Austrian-Irish quantum physicist, who has contributed A LOT in the field. Now who is his cat? The cat doesn’t exist, or does it? Schrodinger made an analogy. He has a cat in a box and he is either feeding it and keeping it alive, or the cat is dead. We don’t know it. Quantum physics is summarized in this one simple analogy. We can never know the pattern or momentum, or even if we do, we won’t be able to measure it. Because if we do, we lose the momentum as well(better explained in Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle).

If we see if the cat is dead, we lose the whole point of the experiment, or do we(see what I did there)? We can use the data to graph, or more simply observe to come up with solutions and conclude whether our “experiment” worked or not. In this case, Schrodinger’s “experiment” worked. More people understand quantum physics just because of this experiment.

How is this useful? Superpositioning, my friends. This is better explained in the next part, Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle.

Heisenberg’s Uncertainty Principle:-

This is the main part. Let’s take the movement of a photon. Let’s also assume that the photon is moving in one straight direction that we don’t know about. Now, let’s measure it. We’ll find the measurement, but we won’t be able to conclude because we also need to find the momentum. Let’s assume we capture it using a motion blur, but hey, we lost its position. It could be anywhere, but it doesn’t matter anymore.

We can never have a photon(in quantum scale), in a definite position with its momentum or how it came there.

Let’s take an easier example that you might be familiar with. The infamous and the most hated(because it creates more harder parts) experiment ever:- The double slit experiment.

Take a lot of photons and shoot it to a wall with an obstacle in front of it. That obstacle has 2 slits, hence the name. If we shoot the photons one by one, we should be expecting 2 lines, right? Never. Photons, or anything in quantum physics, are waves. So they behave like waves and go in a specific position not measured or can’t be measured. Now since there are 2 slits, the pattern overlaps, creating a pattern.



Conclusion:-

Quantum physics is, well… weirder than classic physics. You can never get exact measurements, like never. This obviously is the difference between classical physics and quantum physics. How The String Theory and The Quantum Field Theory Complement Each Other:-

In this paper, we will be talking about how String Theory and QFT(Quantum Field Theory) can complement each other, even if it seems like they don’t. Before diving deep, we shall first know what String Theory and QFT really are. Understanding what these really are, helps us to better understand and reason as to how they can contradict or complement. This paper will show that although there are differences between these two, and are very non-intuitive and can be quite contradicting, these can be complementing, and helping us understand our world better, and perhaps even faster.

String theory, in particle physics, a theory that attempts to merge quantum mechanics with Albert Einstein’s general theory of relativity. The name string theory comes from the modeling of subatomic particles as tiny one-dimensional “stringlike” entities rather than the more conventional approach in which they are modeled as zero-dimensional point particles (source: Britannica). In this theory, the smallest particles are supposedly made up of strings, sometimes referred to as “waves”. These strings are then shown in graphs by even more strings. In Quantum Physics, when data is given in waves, it means that the higher the “height” or amplitude, the more chances of that particle being present at that spot. So, whenever data reveals a wave, it shows the probabilities of strings, or “waves” being at certain spots of the area of measurement. For the rest of the article, the only necessary information needed is that the most elementary particles(subatomic particles) are in the form of strings.

Quantum Field Theory (QFT) is one of the foundational pillars of modern physics, providing a framework to understand how particles, energy, and forces emerge from more fundamental entities known as fields. Unlike classical mechanics, where objects exist as discrete entities in space and interact directly, QFT posits that the universe is composed of continuous fields that permeate every point in spacetime. At each spacetime point, these fields have specific values or, more precisely, quantum operators. Particles, the entities we can detect and measure, are not fundamental; they arise as quantized excitations of these fields. This perspective radically shifts our understanding of matter and energy: rather than being fundamental substances themselves, particles are manifestations of field dynamics. This changes things when it comes to complimenting String Theory with QFT.

In String Theory, “waves” mean the strings that are elementary to subatomic particles. However, in QFT, excitations in these fields are what we often conceptualize as waves. In String Theory, the “strings”, or waves are the particles themselves. They cannot “co-exist” with QFT’s fields. In standard Quantum Physics, waves are used to represent data and observations in graphs. If one adopts String Theory as the deeper theory, we can absolutely try to understand how both—String Theory and QFT—complement each other.

When String Theory is adopted as something deeper than QFT, we can then refer to its particles as “strings” for simplifications, and “waves” for the fields in QFT. In some form of data, strings can be shown as excitations in the form of waves in fields. However, as discussed earlier, we only have a probability that some particular string is somewhere in a measurement area. We could then do that math and find out one position for this one particle, and with that, we have officially merged both String Theory and QFT, into one form of data.

In conclusion, to complement these contradicting theories, one must accept String Theory as a greater theory, and shall proceed to finally merge these 2, into one form of data.

In basketball, it is between LeBron and Jordan. Why? These players are considered to be the best because of their skill, right? Not exactly. These sportspeople have some of the best stats in their respective sports. They are considered the best, because they look the best on paper. People debate that Muhammad Ali is the best boxer of all time, when people debate on Usyk as well. Some debate that it is Anthony Joshua. Anthony Joshua doesn’t have the best stats (although he does have impressive stats).

Why do people debate that Joshua is better than Usyk or even Ali? Eye test. What is the eye test, you may ask, and I have a good answer to that. The eye test is a process of watching athletes in their sports and determining talents by how good or impressive or beautifully they play their sport. Scouts use the eye test to find the best players for their team, and this is the reason why the best isn’t usually signed. Stats, especially in sports like soccer or motorsports, don’t mean much. They do mean a lot, however how good a player plays are never determined entirely by his or her stats. A soccer player might have brilliant defending, yet not many clean sheets. A driver might have the best driving style, yet not the most wins.

One scout said: “A player needs to be seen in person because there is way more to a player than data and analytics.” Another added that data “helps validate what I already see with my eyes,” implying the eye test is foundational. Stats are helpful, but how the stats are earned, usually matters more. Does the eye test always find the best talent? No, not always. However, it is very beneficial to scouts and is also a giant reason why scouts still look at the athletes in person, rather than just relying on stats.

The eye test is the most foundational skill a scout can ever have, and that has dictated many of the recent signings in sports. Statistics give scouts the data to really know whether they can trust the athlete to be consistent or not, but it really is the eye test that dictates most. It is never that one method is better than the other, however it is more that the eye test can complement statistics, and vice versa. The “best” categorization can vary from person to person, or scout to scout, but it is never the same for everyone.

He found an answer
That many would stutter
To talk about it,
But he did it.

Sooner, he got more
This made his reputation roar.
He did the impossible
By making it possible
Dedication,
Was his medication,
To get more
Of his roar

He accomplished a lot
Making anyone go “Oh mein gott”
He stood out
Not by clout,

But by work he contributed
That got him reputed,
More and more,
Building the lore.

All he did was thinking,
Which was everything
He always gave,
Making him brave.

His story is fascinating,
Of just thinking,
What a greek could achieve
Which made everyone leave.

Leave did many
But only the field of philosophy.
He was very good, indeed
Made himself lead.

And soon did he die,
His work did fly.
He was a legend
And it was the end.

This inspired many,
Even the tiny.
Everyone with his ideas would grow…
After all, he was Plato.
